Chassis Lead (January 2025 - Present)

Transition to Autodesk Alias

During the start of the design cycle for the UC'25 vehicle, I lead the team in the transition from our previous surfacing tool, Autodesk Inventor, to using Autodesk Alias, the industry standard tool for modeling of organic and automotive design. The main reason that I pushed for the team to do this was for adaptability and alignment with industry.

For example, one of the main benefits that Alias presents in contrast to inventor is ease-of-analysis. If we see that our design is creating too much drag, or maybe a wheel cutout is too big, in Alias we can simply move the surface over to accomodate for this or any similar design change. However, in Inventor this would require remaking the entire vehicle from scratch.

This is where the benefit of construction history comes in. In Alias, we can have the program remember the previous alterations, surface features, or most operations that depend on one another. As a result, the entire design is fully manipulable. Aside from this, Alias also allows our team to make our molds easier to manufacture, and also allow us to iterate quicker. This, in total, leads to a more aerodynamic and more efficient car.

Currently, Alias is now a team-standardized design product for the aerodynamic design of our car, and remains a fixture in our day-to-day work on the Chassis subteam.

AirCat Pneuamtic Tools

During my time as lead, I have also had the pleasure of being able to secure and maintain a partnership with AirCat Pneumatic Tools, one of the leading pneumatic tool manufacturers in the aerospace industry. With their suppoort, the Chassis team has been able to significnatly reduce our mold manufacturing time, and also allowing us to polish our molds to a 3000+ grit surface finish, resulting in clear-coat like finishes of our carbon fiber parts directly out of the mold.

GitHub for Project Organization

In order to keep track of the various manufacturing, research, and other various tasks that the Chassis team needs to keep track of, I architected the team's first GitHub project tracking system for our subteam. I was inspired by my work at the Italian Institute of Technology this summer (2025), where the lab used the GitHub projects system as standard across every team.

Currently, the system is split into two main repositories. The first is our general tracking repository, where the team keeps track of all tasks that need to be done. The second repsitory that we use is for documentation, aptly named chassis-docs. This was based off of my current note taking system based in obsidian, where we structure our documentation as a knowledge network, in order to faciliate the best retention and organization (in my opinion). At the moment, each member works in their own branch, and the team completes a documentation review using GitHub pull requests before it is merged into the main branch, so that we keep a level of quality across our repository.

Website Designer (August 2025 - Present)

In addition to my more engineering-focused roles, I also designed and coded the new website for our team, porting the old styling choices from the previous site. The reason for remaking the site this way was twofold: being able to have ownership of our own data, and be able to standardize version control for our site. As Git/version control software is industry standard, I wanted to make sure that any member with relevant experience could suggest their own edits to the site, so that we could improve it as much as possible. Furthermore, transitioning from Wix to NextJS yielded a significantly faster, and cleaner website, and one that we could choose where to host. Finally, the extensibiilty that pure JavaScript provides means that the site is able to adapt to any future framework that might come up in the future. Also, with the introduction of Agentic AI into most modern code editors, even team members with very little coding experience can make very large changes to the site.
